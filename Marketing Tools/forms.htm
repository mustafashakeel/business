Tips for completing forms

Forms list

This section lists all the forms supplied in this kit, with brief notes about each.

There is no set order in which the forms should be used! Nor are there “right” and “wrong” answers. Most of the forms listed below are designed to help you assemble key information in ways that should make it easier to analyse. A few additional forms are included to help you in your day-to-day operations.

Important: Documents created with Microsoft Word do not always display reliably in a web browser. For that reason, we have not linked to Word-format documents in the table below. To open the Word-format documents, look in the folder created on your Windows desktop during the installation of this kit.

Notes
Activities and Budgets

Enter all the major tasks you will need to do to get your business started. This may include finding an appropriate location, developing a marketing plan, signing a lease, and purchasing equipment. Assign a date to complete each task and estimate how much money each task will cost.
The Worksheets

Before you take that first big step and start your own business, answer all the questions in Worksheets 1 through 4 to make sure that you have thought about everything and researched everything, and that you are fully prepared to “make it” in the business world.
Worksheet 1

Worksheet 1 focuses on your motivations, aptitude, and business plan.
Worksheet 2

Without a thorough analysis of the market you plan to enter, your new business could be in danger of failing right from the beginning. Worksheet 2 will help you identify some important issues in your marketplace, and it will help you double-check if there are crucial issues that you may not have considered before you start your new business.
Worksheet 3

This worksheet will help you to identify the best possible location for your business. If you have already chosen a location, answering these questions will help you to turn that location into the perfect environment!
Worksheet 4

Answering the questions in Worksheet 4 will help you to identify a marketing strategy for your business. It will bring out the creative side in you by helping you to generate some great ideas for marketing your new business!
Forecasting the Future

Depending on your type of business and the amount of money it will cost to get it started, try to predict when your break-even point will be. The break-even point is the time when you have generated enough profits from your business to cover all the start-up costs. Then, think of all the factors that might affect your break-even point in the first year of business, and in the future.

Industry Trends

The business world today changes so quickly, it’s important to identify possible changes in your industry to ensure that your business will be flexible enough to overcome changes and succeed. These questions will help you identify any possible industry trends that may benefit or threaten the success of your business.
Invoice Product

Template for an invoice for products.
Invoice Service

Template for an invoice for services rendered.
Marketing Budget

This worksheet will help you determine a cost for your marketing budget. In the first year you begin your business, your marketing budget may be higher than in subsequent years because you may find it necessary to carve a niche in the market.
Marketing Calendar

Use this template to plan and track your marketing program.
Marketing Plan

An outline-style template to write your marketing plan.
Company Objectives

To help you identify goals for your new company over the first year and subsequent years, identify objectives you might want to achieve. Be as specific as possible. And for each objective, make a note of how you might achieve that goal.
Outside Influences

There are many things that can affect your new business, some of which are beyond your control. It’s useful to have a good grasp of what these influences might be, especially for the first year of operation, so that you can prepare for them.
Personal Budget

This worksheet will help you to determine how much income you will need to generate from your new business in order to satisfy your personal needs and your family’s needs.
Personal & Professional Objectives

Before you start a business, it can be helpful to identify what is important to you in life — both in your personal life and business life. Since running your own business can take up a great deal of your time, you may want to explore how that might affect your life and how you might alter your business for both personal and financial success!
Outside Influences - P.E.S.T.

This is a standard test that every business should do at least once a year. It’s especially important if you are starting a business because it will help you to identify some of the factors that will affect your business that you might have no control over. However, when you identify these influences, try to think of ways that you can overcome these challenges.
Purchase Order

Template to produce your own purchase orders.
Monthly Income Record

Simple form for tracking sources of revenue each month.
Start-up Costs

Form for recording and identifying start-up expenses for a new business.
Competition Analysis

Before you open your new business, you should conduct a thorough comparison of the competition. This worksheet will help you to identify not only how much competition there is, but also how you can manage your company to excel in areas where the competition fails.
Internal Influences - S.W.O.T.

This is a great exercise to assess the strengths and weaknesses of your new business. You can do this before you open your business by considering the strengths and weakness of your business model. Once you have been operating for a year, review and update it every 12 months. As you do this exercise, you might notice that some strengths can also be seen as weaknesses, and some threats can also be seen as opportunities.
